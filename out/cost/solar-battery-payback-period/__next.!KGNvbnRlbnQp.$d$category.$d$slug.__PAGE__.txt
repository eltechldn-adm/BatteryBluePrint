c:T8ee,<p>The "Payback Period" is the holy grail metric for solar finance.
It answers: <em>How long until this system has saved me enough cash to pay for its initial price tag?</em></p>
<p>For solar panels, this is easy (usually 4–6 years).
For batteries, it is complicated.</p>
<p>Complex utility rates, inflation, and degradation mean a simple "back of napkin" calculation is usually wrong. Here is how to do it properly.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-formula"><a href="#the-formula">The Formula</a></h2>
<p>The basic formula for Simple Payback is:</p>
<p><code>Net Cost ($)</code> / <code>Annual Savings ($)</code> = <code>Years to Payback</code>.</p>
<h3 id="1-calculating-net-cost"><a href="#1-calculating-net-cost">1. Calculating Net Cost</a></h3>
<p>This is the easy part.
<code>Total Quote Price</code> - <code>Tax Credits/Rebates</code> = <code>Net Cost</code>.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Example (US):</strong> Quote is $15,000. 30% Tax Credit is $4,500.</li>
<li><strong>Net Cost:</strong> $10,500.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="2-calculating-annual-savings-the-hard-part"><a href="#2-calculating-annual-savings-the-hard-part">2. Calculating Annual Savings (The Hard Part)</a></h3>
<p>Your battery saves money in two ways:</p>
<p><strong>A. Bill Reduction (Time-of-Use Arbitrage)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Scenario: Use battery power instead of Grid Power during peak hours.</li>
<li>Math: <code>(Peak Rate - OffPeak Rate)</code> × <code>kWh cycled per year</code>.</li>
<li>Example: ($0.50 - $0.10) × (10 kWh × 365 days) = <strong>$1,460 / year</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>B. Export Optimization (Solar Self-Consumption)</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Scenario: Instead of selling solar for pennies ($0.04), you store it and use it to avoid buying grid power ($0.30).</li>
<li>Math: <code>(Import Price - Export Price)</code> × <code>kWh stored per year</code>.</li>
<li>Example: ($0.30 - $0.04) × (3,000 kWh/yr) = <strong>$780 / year</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<p>You need to sum A + B to get your total annual benefit.</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Note:</strong> You generally cannot do both A and B with the same kWh. You usually calculate one primary strategy.</p>
</blockquote>
<hr>
<h2 id="worked-example-uk-home-high-roi"><a href="#worked-example-uk-home-high-roi">Worked Example: UK Home (High ROI)</a></h2>
d:Tbde,
<p>The UK market currently offers some of the best paybacks in the world due to "Smart Tariffs" like Octopus Flux.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>System:</strong> 9.5 kWh Battery (GivEnergy).</li>
<li><strong>Installed Cost:</strong> £6,000 (0% VAT).</li>
<li><strong>Strategy:</strong> "Force Charge" cheap electricity at night (02:00-05:00) @ 9p/kWh. Discharge during peak (16:00-19:00) avoiding 35p/kWh import.
<ul>
<li>Spread: 26p per kWh.</li>
<li>Cycle: 9.5 kWh daily.</li>
<li>Daily Save: £2.47</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Annual Savings:</strong> £2.47 × 365 = £901.</li>
<li><strong>Payback:</strong> £6,000 / £901 = <strong>6.6 Years</strong>.</li>
<li><strong>After Year 6:</strong> Pure profit for the remaining 9 years of warranty.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="worked-example-us-home-california-nem-30"><a href="#worked-example-us-home-california-nem-30">Worked Example: US Home (California NEM 3.0)</a></h2>
<p>California's new rules decimated solar-only value but supercharged batteries.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>System:</strong> Tesla Powerwall 3.</li>
<li><strong>Net Cost:</strong> $11,500 (After 30% Fed Credit).</li>
<li><strong>Strategy:</strong> Avoid importing power from 4pm-9pm ($0.60/kWh summer). Store solar that would otherwise be sold for $0.04.
<ul>
<li>Value created: ~$0.50 per kWh.</li>
<li>Annual Savings: ~$1,800.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Payback:</strong> $11,500 / $1,800 = <strong>6.4 Years</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-inflation-factor-why-simple-payback-is-conservative"><a href="#the-inflation-factor-why-simple-payback-is-conservative">The Inflation Factor: Why "Simple Payback" is Conservative</a></h2>
<p>The math above assumes electricity prices stay flat for 10 years. They won't.
Utility rates historically rise 3–5% per year.</p>
<ul>
<li>Year 1 Savings: $1,000.</li>
<li>Year 5 Savings: $1,200 (due to higher grid rates).</li>
<li>Year 10 Savings: $1,500.</li>
</ul>
<p>In reality, your payback accelerates every time the utility company raises their rates. A "7 Year" simple payback is often a "5.5 Year" real-world payback when adjusted for inflation.</p>
