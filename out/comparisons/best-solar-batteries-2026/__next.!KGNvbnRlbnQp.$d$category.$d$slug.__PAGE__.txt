<h2 id="1-tesla-powerwall-3-the-standard"><a href="#1-tesla-powerwall-3-the-standard">1. Tesla Powerwall 3 (The Standard)</a></h2>
e:T33fe,
<p>The Apple of batteries. Ubiquitous, sleek, and high-performance.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Chemistry:</strong> LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate). <em>Note: Upgrade from PW2's NMC.</em></li>
<li><strong>Capacity:</strong> 13.5 kWh.</li>
<li><strong>Power Output:</strong> 11.5 kW (Continuous). <em>Massive power output.</em></li>
<li><strong>Integration:</strong> Built-in Hybrid Solar Inverter.</li>
<li><strong>Best For:</strong> New installs where you want high power to start AC units.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Incredible 11.5 kW output eliminates the need for "soft starters" on AC units.</li>
<li>Best app interface in the industry.</li>
<li>Integrated solar inverter saves money on new installs.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Customer service is famously difficult to reach.</li>
<li>"Walled Garden" ecosystem.</li>
</ul>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Winner for Power:</strong> 11.5 kW continuous makes this the only battery that can start central AC units without soft starters.</p>
</blockquote>

<h2 id="2-franklinwh-apower-the-retrofit-king"><a href="#2-franklinwh-apower-the-retrofit-king">2. FranklinWH aPower (The Retrofit King)</a></h2>
<p>A favorite among electricians. It is built to be robust, generator-friendly, and chemistry-agnostic.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Chemistry:</strong> LFP.</li>
<li><strong>Capacity:</strong> 13.6 kWh.</li>
<li><strong>Power Output:</strong> 5 kW (Continuous) / 10 kW (Peak).</li>
<li><strong>Integration:</strong> AC Coupled (Works with any solar brand).</li>
<li><strong>Best For:</strong> Whole-home backup with generator integration.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>The "Gatekeeper" Architecture:</strong>
FranklinWH's secret weapon is its "Gate" smart panel. It has a dedicated input for a <strong>Gas Generator</strong>.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Scenario:</strong> Grid dies. Battery drains.</li>
<li><strong>Action:</strong> The Gate automatically starts your gas generator to recharge the battery and power the house, then shuts it off.</li>
<li><strong>Verdict:</strong> The ultimate off-grid resilience setup.</li>
</ul>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Winner for Resilience:</strong> The only battery with seamless generator integration. Critical for multi-week outages.</p>
</blockquote>

<h2 id="3-enphase-iq-battery-5p-the-modular-choice"><a href="#3-enphase-iq-battery-5p-the-modular-choice">3. Enphase IQ Battery 5P (The Modular Choice)</a></h2>
<p>Enphase dominates the microinverter market, and their battery is designed to sync perfectly with it.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Chemistry:</strong> LFP.</li>
<li><strong>Capacity:</strong> 5 kWh (Modular - stack as many as you want).</li>
<li><strong>Power Output:</strong> 3.84 kW (per 5kWh unit).</li>
<li><strong>Integration:</strong> AC Coupled (Distributed architecture).</li>
<li><strong>Best For:</strong> Current Enphase solar owners.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Redundancy:</strong> If one battery unit fails, the others keep working.</li>
<li><strong>Burst Power:</strong> Two 5P units (10 kWh) deliver 7.68kW continuous—very high power density.</li>
<li><strong>Wired Comms:</strong> Uses wired communication, more reliable than Zigbee/WiFi.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Expensive per kWh compared to Tesla.</li>
<li>Wall space: Mounting 4 separate units takes up a lot of garage wall.</li>
</ul>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Winner for Modularity:</strong> 15-year warranty and unlimited stacking make this the most future-proof choice.</p>
</blockquote>

<h2 id="buying-guide-what-specs-actually-matter"><a href="#buying-guide-what-specs-actually-matter">Buying Guide: What specs actually matter?</a></h2>
<p>Ignore the marketing fluff. Look at these 3 numbers.</p>
<h3 id="1-continuous-power-output-kw"><a href="#1-continuous-power-output-kw">1. Continuous Power Output (kW)</a></h3>
<p>This is "How many things can I run at once?"</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>5 kW:</strong> You can run the fridge, lights, TV, and microwave. NOT the AC.</li>
<li><strong>7 kW:</strong> You can run small AC units.</li>
<li><strong>10 kW+:</strong> You can run central AC and electric ovens.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="2-usable-capacity-kwh"><a href="#2-usable-capacity-kwh">2. Usable Capacity (kWh)</a></h3>
<p>This is "How long will it last?"</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>10 kWh:</strong> Minimal backup (Lights/Fridge overnight).</li>
<li><strong>20-30 kWh:</strong> Whole home backup for 24 hours.</li>
<li><strong>Rule of Thumb:</strong> You usually need 2 batteries (26 kWh) to feel "Normal" during an outage.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="3-chemistry-lfp-vs-nmc"><a href="#3-chemistry-lfp-vs-nmc">3. Chemistry (LFP vs NMC)</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate):</strong> Safer, longer lasting (6000+ cycles), no thermal runaway risk. <strong>Buy this.</strong></li>
<li><strong>NMC (Nickel Manganese Cobalt):</strong> Older tech (Powerwall 2). Higher energy density but lower lifespan. <strong>Avoid unless discounted.</strong></li>
</ul>
